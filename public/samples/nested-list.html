A. Legislative Branch — Makes Laws

  1. Congress
    • Bicameral legislature:
      • Senate: 100 members (2 per state)
      • House of Representatives: 435 members (based on population)
  2. Powers of Congress
    • Passes federal laws and budgets.
    • Declares war.
    • Regulates interstate and foreign commerce.
    • Can impeach and remove federal officials.
  3. Checks on Other Branches
    • Can override presidential vetoes (with 2/3 vote).
    • Approves presidential appointments and treaties.
    • Controls federal spending.

B. Executive Branch — Enforces Laws

  1. President
    • Head of state and government.
    • Commander-in-chief of the armed forces.
    • Signs or vetoes legislation.
    • Appoints cabinet members, federal judges, and ambassadors.
    • Conducts foreign policy and negotiates treaties.
  2. Vice President
    • President of the Senate (casts tie-breaking votes).
    • Assumes presidency if the President cannot serve.
  3. The Cabinet
    • Advisors to the President.
    • Includes heads of 15 executive departments (e.g., Defense, State, Treasury).
  4. Executive Agencies
    • Independent agencies and commissions (e.g., EPA, NASA, FBI).

C. Judicial Branch — Interprets Laws

  1. Supreme Court
    • Highest court in the U.S.
    • Nine justices (one Chief Justice, eight Associate Justices).
    • Reviews constitutionality of laws and government actions.
  2. Lower Federal Courts
    • Courts of Appeals (13 circuits).
    • District Courts (94 total).
  3. Judicial Review

Established by Marbury v. Madison* (1803).
