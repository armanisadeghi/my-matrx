The Regulatory Landscape in Orange County

California imposes some of the nation's strictest requirements on IT asset disposal. Two areas demand immediate attention from any Orange County organization retiring IT equipment.

E-Waste Disposal

The Electronic Waste Recycling Act (SB 20/SB 1215) prohibits landfill disposal of computers, monitors, and other covered devices. All end-of-life electronics must be handled as Universal Waste under DTSC regulations — authorized recyclers, labeled containers, documented inventory, and trained personnel. Violations carry DTSC fines and environmental penalties.

SB 20 · SB 1215 · DTSC Universal Waste

Data Destruction

California Civil Code §1798.81 requires businesses to destroy personal information records so they are unreadable and irrecoverable. CCPA/CPRA add further obligations. Regulated industries face additional federal overlaps — HIPAA for healthcare, GLBA for financial services, DFARS/NIST 800-171 for defense contractors. Noncompliance exposes your organization to enforcement actions, breach liability, and reputational damage.

Civil Code §1798.81 · CCPA/CPRA · NIST 800-88

Healthcare & Biotech/Medtech

Irvine · Lake Forest · San Clemente · Laguna Hills · Rancho Santa Margarita · Anaheim
⚠ The Challenge

Orange County's healthcare sector — spanning major hospital systems, medical device manufacturers, biotech research firms, and large physician groups — faces the most stringent data destruction requirements of any industry. HIPAA mandates verifiable, documented destruction of any device that has ever stored, processed, or transmitted electronic protected health information (ePHI). This extends well beyond standard office computers to diagnostic imaging systems, lab workstations, embedded medical devices, patient check-in tablets, and portable equipment. A single unwiped hard drive from a decommissioned MRI controller or pharmacy system can trigger a reportable breach.

✓ Our Solution

All Green offers On-Site Hard Drive Shredding performed at your facility's loading dock or secure area, ensuring ePHI never leaves your custody intact. For large hospital system refreshes and medical device manufacturer cleanouts, we deploy full Enterprise Decommissioning teams with HIPAA-trained personnel. Every asset is inventoried, every drive destroyed to NIST 800-88 standards, and every action documented with serialized Certificates of Destruction that satisfy HHS audit requirements.

Local Experience We proudly serve major hospital networks, medical device manufacturers, and clinical research organizations across Orange County's healthcare corridor, from large Anaheim-area medical centers to specialized biotech campuses in South County.
